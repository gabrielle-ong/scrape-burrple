Rong Shu Xia Bak Kut Teh 榕树下肉骨茶
37 Tai Thong Crescent, Singapore
What's more comforting than a bubbling claypot of JB-style bak kut teh especially on a cold rainy day?

A plate of braised pork belly rice, steamed chicken rice, claypot prawn noodles and a new creation of dry laksa will do the trick! Besides the bak kut teh, which is of a milder herbal version and not peppery kind, they have several options to cater to everyone. Special mention has to go to that dry laksa which comes with huge prawns and slurpy-worthy laksa gravy noodles combination.

Rong Shu Xia Bak Kut Teh 榕树下肉骨茶
37 Tai Thong Crescent, Singapore
Claypot Bak Kut Teh For 2

Claypot Bak Kut Teh ($9.90 for 2 pax)

This non-traditional BKT's soup base was neither the extremely peppery or the richly herbal type of broth. Goodies in the pot included pork ribs, meatballs, pork belly slices and button mushrooms. While I cannot complain about having a variety of ingredients in my BKT, I felt that this diluted the rich brothy taste that is synonymous with the more famous BKT stalls. Nevertheless, I love how this month-old shop offers its customers a huge array of side options at economical prices your wallets will thank you for.

Also in picture were the Pig Intestines ($2.90), Garlic Pork ($4.80) and Dough Fritters aka You Tiao ($1.50). NB: only one refill of soup per claypot 😐.

Rong Shu Xia Bak Kut Teh 榕树下肉骨茶
37 Tai Thong Crescent, Singapore
Fried Laksa ($5.90)

Who thought a bak kut teh shop would be selling such cool dishes like this? 🍲

This Fried Laksa was the most fun and exciting plate on our table at Rong Shu Xia Bak Kut Teh. Wet and quite lemak, it's kinda like hokkien mee but mixed up with the spicy coconutty flavors (and guilt) of laksa. 😂

I was quite underwhelmed by the actual bak kut teh though, which didn't pack that herbal kick you'd expect from the JB-style BKT. 😣 But at those prices and variety, I'm sure everyone can find something they'd like!
